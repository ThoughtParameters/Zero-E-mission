# Atmospheric Water Generation (AWG)

## What Is Atmospheric Water Generation?

Atmospheric Water Generation (AWG) is the process of extracting potable water directly from
the moisture present in ambient air. Rather than relying on rainfall, rivers, groundwater
wells, or municipal supply infrastructure, AWG systems harvest the humidity that exists
everywhere on Earth—even in arid and semi-arid regions—and convert it into clean, drinkable
water.

The process mimics nature's dew cycle:

1. **Intake** – Ambient air is drawn into the system by a fan or blower.
2. **Condensation** – The air passes over a cooled surface (or through a desiccant medium)
   where water vapor condenses into liquid droplets.
3. **Collection & Filtration** – Condensed water is collected, then passed through multi-stage
   filters (sediment, activated carbon, UV sterilisation) to remove contaminants.
4. **Storage & Dispensing** – Purified water is held in a food-grade tank and dispensed
   on demand.

### Core Technologies

| Technology | How It Works | Best Climate |
|---|---|---|
| **Refrigerant-based cooling** | A compressor cools a heat-exchanger coil below the dew point; moisture condenses on the coil | Humid (RH > 40 %) |
| **Desiccant-based (solid/liquid)** | Hygroscopic material absorbs vapour; heat regenerates the material and releases liquid water | Arid & dry climates |
| **Radiative cooling** | Surfaces radiate heat to the sky at night, dropping below dew point passively | Low-cost off-grid applications |
| **Thermoelectric (Peltier)** | Solid-state cooling using DC current; no moving parts | Small-scale / portable |

## Why AWG Matters for Zero-Emission Infrastructure

AWG systems are a key pillar of resilient, off-grid water supply because:

- **No pipeline or borehole infrastructure** is required, reducing capital cost and
  construction disruption.
- **Water quality is consistently high** because filtration is built into the unit rather
  than depending on a distribution network.
- **They pair naturally with renewable energy** – a solar photovoltaic array or small wind
  turbine can power an AWG unit around the clock (see
  [Solar Photovoltaic and Hydrogen Power Generation](./solar-photovoltaic-hydrogen-power.md)).
- **They are scalable** – multiple units can be deployed in parallel to match community growth.
- **They reduce reliance on surface or groundwater**, helping to protect local aquifers
  from over-extraction.

## Key Considerations and Limitations

| Factor | Impact |
|---|---|
| **Relative humidity** | Output drops significantly below 30 % RH; desiccant variants mitigate this |
| **Ambient temperature** | Cold air holds less moisture; output is reduced in cold climates |
| **Energy consumption** | Refrigerant-based units are energy-intensive; renewable pairing is recommended |
| **Maintenance** | Filters must be replaced periodically; UV lamps require annual service |
| **Water mineralisation** | RO removes beneficial minerals; post-treatment mineralisation may be needed |
